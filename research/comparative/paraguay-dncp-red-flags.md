# Paraguay DNCP red flags

## Snapshot

DNCP already defines public red flags and has described a larger internal red-flag universe. Paraguay also exposes OCDS-oriented procurement data and related experimental tools such as R3D.

## Strong ideas

- local institutional legitimacy
- public explanation that flags indicate conduct requiring investigation, not confirmed misconduct
- precedent for recurrence, PAC publication, and comparative price indicators
- real operational need for internal and public risk tools

## Limits for Paraguay

- public DNCP indicators are only part of the wider risk universe.
- Centinela should not pretend to replace the institution's role or internal knowledge.

## Adopt now

- local terminology and caution
- explicit respect for Paraguay's existing procurement-integrity infrastructure
- eventual mapping between DNCP public flags and Centinela's rule registry

## Defer or adapt

- copying DNCP indicators blindly without documenting assumptions and gaps

## Role in Centinela

DNCP is the key local precedent. It ensures the Paraguay-first system remains grounded in the country's institutional reality.
